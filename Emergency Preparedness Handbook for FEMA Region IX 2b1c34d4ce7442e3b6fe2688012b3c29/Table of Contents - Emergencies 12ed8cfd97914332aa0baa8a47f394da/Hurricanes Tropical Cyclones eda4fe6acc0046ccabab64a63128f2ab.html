What are Hurricanes / Tropical Cyclones?

In short, hurricanes are large swirling storms formed over oceans. Another name for a hurricane is a tropical cyclone, the only difference being that hurricanes refer to the large tropical storms in the Caribbean, the Gulf of Mexico, and Atlantic Oceans while tropical cyclones refer to the tropical storms that form in the Northwest Pacific Ocean. This page will refer to hurricanes and tropical cyclones solely as hurricanes from here on out. These storms bring great amounts of rainfall and wind, causing widespread chaos and destruction for its victims. NASA.gov states that there are three main parts of a hurricane, those being:

  • The Eye
    • This is the center of the hurricane. Interestingly, there are usually light winds and almost, if not totally clear skies.
  • The Eye Wall
    • The eye wall of a hurricane is perhaps one of the most dangerous elements of the hurricane. The winds are heavy and the winds are strong in this wall. These storm circle around the eye of the storm.
  • The Rain Bands
    • These are bands of clouds and rain that extend out from a hurricane's eye wall. These bring with it thunderstorms, and if wind conditions are strong enough, tornadoes.

Why are Hurricanes Dangerous?

Hurricanes generate storm surges, which is a rise of seawater during a storm. These can, like 🌊Tsunamis, overpower and destroy swaths of buildings. These pose a major threat to human survival. Flash Floods / Floods usually follow hurricanes. 🔌Power Outages can also accompany hurricanes. Furthermore, wind levels can end up being dangerously high

How Can You Prepare for a Hurricane?

There are quite a few things you can do to prepare for a hurricane. According to Ready.gov, you can:

  • Learn evacuation routes.
  • Constantly monitor weather patterns and bulletins from weather.gov and NOAA.
  • Purchase hurricane insurance

Be sure to prepare with your family by:

  • Figuring out potential evacuation routes.
  • Gathering essential objects:
    • Food
    • Medication
    • Water
    • Cleaning supplies
      • Soap
      • Hand sanitizer
      • Disinfecting wipes
      • General cleaning supplies
    • First-aid tools
    • A battery-operated radio
    • A fully charged phone
    • Consider boarding up windows to minimize property damage.
    • Consider building a wind-safe room.

In any case, you should create a contact list, with out of state contacts.

What Can You Do During A Hurricane?

There are a few things you can do during a hurricane. Provided that you have monitored the necessary communication channels, officials will announce if an evacuation is needed. If such an order is announced, listen to it and move towards safe ground. Be sure to take essential items with you.

If you do find yourself in flooding, attempt to find higher ground.

  • If indoors,
    • Do not climb into closed spaces, as the rising flood water might trap you.
    • If in a building, try to climb to the highest point in the building.
  • If outside,
    • Do not try to go AGAINST the flood water. Ready.gov recommends that you "Turn Around, Don't Drown!"
    • Avoid bridges that are over fast-moving waters.
  • If it is safe to do so, listen to radio and/or TV for information.
  • Turn off utilities if officials ask for it. If not, be sure to turn your refrigerator's thermostat to the lowest degree and keep its doors closed.

How Can You Be Safe After A Hurricane?

After a hurricane be sure to:

  • Keep listening to radio or continue listening to the news.
  • Monitor official communication channels for instructions on returning back to your home, if you were evacuated.
  • Inspect your home for any damage.
    • Be sure to wear protective clothing.
    • Do not touch electrical equipment if you and/or it is wet.
    • If there is damage, document it properly with photographs.
  • Communicate to your contacts that you are safe
