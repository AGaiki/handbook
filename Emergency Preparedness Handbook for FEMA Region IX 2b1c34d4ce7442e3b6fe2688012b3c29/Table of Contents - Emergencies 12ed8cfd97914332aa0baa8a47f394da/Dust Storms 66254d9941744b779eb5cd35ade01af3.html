What are dust storms?

Dust storms are walls of dust and debris that are blown into a particular area by thunderstorms. They are considered to be large, and can blanket large areas of land, disrupting infrastructure such as airliners.

Where do Dust Storms Occur in Region IX?

Unlike most natural disasters, dust storms do not require a particular type of geographic landform. This means that it can occur almost anywhere, if the circumstances are appropriate. In the United States, these commonly occur in the Southwest, in states such as Arizona. However, this can still occur in dry places such as California.

Why are Dust Storms Dangerous?

Even though dust storms can last a mere few minutes, it can make conditions and circumstances dangerous. Wind can cause dust and other particles to concentrate tightly and also allow it be pushed in a particular direction. Due to this, dust storms can:

  • Reduce visibility
  • Cause difficulty with breathing
  • Disrupt infrastructure
  • Cause traffic pileup and dangerous driving conditions.

How Can You Prepare for a Dust Storm?

You should know if you are at risk for dust storms. Commonly, these can occur in agricultural areas, however, they can still occur in urban areas. Make sure to be up to date with:

  • Radio announcements
    • Oftentimes, radio, such as NOAA's Weather Radio, or commercial radio can relay any pertinent information and announcements in regards to a dust storm (or any other potential disasters).
  • Weather alerts:
    • Remain cognizant of any weather alerts issued by government organizations such as the National Weather Service (NWS) or NOAA. You can check for local weather alerts at weather.gov.

Make sure you can prepare yourself by:

  • Preparing your home:
    • Make sure to secure any items outside of your house (such as vases, pots, etc.) C
    • Close doors leading to the outside, and make sure to close windows and shutters / shades / curtains.
    • Trim trees away from your home and nearby power lines to minimize potential damage.

Depending on the severity of the dust storm, officials may even ask you to evacuate. Make sure to monitor all lines of communication before a dust storm in order to receive proper and appropriate information.

What Can You Do During a Dust Storm?

During a dust storm, you can find yourself driving a car or other vehicle on a road. The State of Arizona's Emergency Information Network recommends drivers follow a series of maneuvers, as outlined below:

  • Pull off the road
    • If a dust storm approaches you while you drive, pull off the main lane(s) off the highway (if it is safe to do so). Make sure to shift the car to the parking break.
    • If not possible, continue driving at a speed that is appropriate for the visibility. At the same time, turn on your external car lights and occasionally use your horn in order to alert vehicles around you.
  • Turn off ALL your external car lights
    • Drivers behind you may try to follow the lights of your car to escape the dust storm, and in order to prevent accidents, make sure to turn your external car lights off until the storm passes.

If you do find yourself to be inside during a dust storm, Maricopa County Emergency Management suggests to consider:

  • Closing your doors and windows.
  • Turning off air-conditioners until the dust storm is over.
  • Bring pets indoors.

If you do find yourself outside during a dust storm, consider:

  • Getting as low to the ground. Make sure to be as far away as possible from roads and freeways.
  • Avoiding trees and other low-lying areas. Dust storms are usually followed by severe winds and thunderstorms, which can lead to flash-flooding in certain areas. Make sure to read more about flash-flooding in Flash Floods / Floods.

How Can You Be Safe After a Dust Storm?

After the dust storm is over, be sure to, in any case, listen to authorities about any other hazardous weather conditions. Good sources for this are a local government's emergency office or the National Weather Service.

More specifically, if you are driving, be sure to:

  • Check traffic around you and if all conditions are safe, resume driving tom your destination.
  • Assess other conditions such as traffic jams, light outages, and obstacles in the road. If you notice any downed power lines or damaged roadways, make sure to report them to the proper authorities.
