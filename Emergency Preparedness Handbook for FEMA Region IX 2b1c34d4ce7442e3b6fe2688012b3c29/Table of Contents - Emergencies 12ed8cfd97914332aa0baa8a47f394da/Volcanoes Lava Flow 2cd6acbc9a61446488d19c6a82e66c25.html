What are Volcanoes and Lava Flow?

According to Ready.gov, a volcano is an opening in the Earth's crust which provides an opening for the flow of molten rock (lava), debris, and various gases. A volcanic eruption can include lava flow, which can flow at high speeds and cause destruction to anything in its path. There are approximately 169 potentially active volcanoes in the entirety of the United States according to the USGS.

Where do Volcanic Eruptions and Lava Flow occur in Region IX?

According to the USGS, the United States is ranked 3rd in the world for the number of historically active volcanoes. Why are Volcanos and Lava Flow Dangerous?

Since volcanic eruptions are associated with heavy ash and gas flowing from the volcano, volcanic eruptions can:

  • Reduce visibility
  • Cause difficulty with breathing
  • Contaminate water supplies
  • Damage structures and machines

Lava flow from volcanoes can also be highly destructive and dangerous, as lava can reach extreme temperatures.

How Can You Prepare for a Volcanic Eruption?

Know the risk in your area:

  • Talk with your local emergency management about evacuation plans, shelter plans, and details on protection from a volcanic eruption.
  • Talk with your insurance to find out what is covered in the event of a damaging volcanic eruption.
  • Be aware of any emergency alert system in your area for volcanic eruptions and sign up.
    • See the Volcano Notification Service provided by the USGS.
  • Talk with your doctor about any health conditions you may have that can increase breathing difficulty during a volcanic eruption.

Prepare for yourself and family:

  • Have and practice a communication and evacuation plan with your family and pets.
  • If possible, stock up on non-perishable food, water, batteries, necessary medication, and cleaning supplies.
  • Have a plan in case you need to shelter in place.
  • Make sure any important documents, such as your ID and insurance papers, are kept in a secure place.

What Can You Do During a Volcanic Eruption?

Keep up to date:

  • Listen and be aware of any alerts.
  • Follow shelter or evacuation orders, and make note of shelters nearby to evacuate quickly.

Protect yourself:

  • Avoid going to areas downstream of the volcanic eruption, such as river valleys.
  • Limit ash exposure by doing the following:
    • Avoid driving in heavy ash.
    • Avoid going outdoors.
    • Take temporary shelter if you have the supplies.
    • Do not try to remove ash from your property.
    • Seal doors and windows and cover any ventilator openings.
    • Wear a respirator facemask, such as a N95, for long periods of exposure, and wear a simple cloth mask for short periods of exposure. Please note a cloth mask will not protect you from ash for a long period of time like a respirator will.

How Can You Be Safe After a Volcanic Eruption?

Keep yourself safe:

  • Do not try to remove ash unless if you have guidance.
    • If you need to remove ash, be careful of the slippery surface and wear proper cleaning clothes and a proper mask to keep yourself safe from the ash.
  • Continue to avoid driving in heavy ash to prevent engine damage, and to limit exposure to ash especially if you have breathing difficulty.

Keep in the know:

  • Reach out to family and friends through social media or text messages, since phone systems can be busy.
  • Be aware of any notices or updates from local authorities.
